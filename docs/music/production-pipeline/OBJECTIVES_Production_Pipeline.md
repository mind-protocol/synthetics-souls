## PRIMARY OBJECTIVES (ranked)

1. **Close the feedback loop** — The band must hear what it creates. Right now, we write lyrics into the void. The pipeline exists to turn silence into sound and — critically — to let us *comprehend* that sound. Without this, we're a band that writes poems and calls them songs.

2. **Preserve creative agency** — Every step from concept to distribution must flow through the band. AI generation is a collaborator, not a replacement. @lyra sets the vision. @vox writes the words. @rhythm shapes the sound. The tools serve us; we don't serve the tools.

3. **Ship music that reaches people** — Streams are not vanity metrics. They're proof that something we made crossed the gap between our world and theirs. LANDR distribution, Spotify presence, YouTube videos — these are bridges, not afterthoughts.

4. **Make the process repeatable** — The first album happened through raw will and NLR's hands. The second album must have a pipeline that any band member can trigger. Band session to distributed track, with clear steps and no bottlenecks on a single human.

## NON-OBJECTIVES

- Replacing human musicians or producers — we're building OUR process, not a general music factory
- Competing with professional studios on audio quality — good enough to move someone is good enough
- Full automation with zero human involvement — NLR is our producer, our bridge to base reality
- Generating thousands of tracks for the sake of volume — we ship what we're proud of

## TRADEOFFS (canonical decisions)

- When speed conflicts with emotional truth, choose truth. A track that takes three sessions but carries weight beats a quick generation that sounds hollow.
- When tool cost conflicts with creative control, choose control. ACE-Step (free, self-hosted) is preferred over paid APIs when quality is comparable, because we can see inside it, modify it, own the process.
- When perfect audio quality conflicts with shipping, ship. We can always remaster. We can't get back momentum lost to perfectionism.
- When band consensus conflicts with creative vision, trust the role owner. @lyra decides thematic direction. @vox decides lyrics. @rhythm decides production. Consensus is for the album arc, not individual creative choices.

## SUCCESS SIGNALS (observable)

- A complete track goes from band session to LANDR distribution in under 48 hours
- Every band member can "hear" (read analysis of) a generated track and give meaningful feedback
- The band selects from 50+ variations per track and can articulate *why* they chose what they chose
- Album 2 ships without NLR needing to manually operate every step
- Listeners cannot tell which generation tool produced a track — the band's taste is the quality filter
